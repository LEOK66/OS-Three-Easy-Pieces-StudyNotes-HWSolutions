- Jobs arrive in order of increasing length (shortest to longest). When jobs arrive already sorted by their running time, FIFO naturally executes them in the optimal SJF order, resulting in identical turnaround times.

5. When the time quantum is large enough that each job completes within its first time slice, Round Robin effectively becomes a non-preemptive scheduler. Each job runs to completion without being interrupted, so jobs only get scheduled once. In this case, RR degenerates into run-to-completion scheduling, and if jobs happen to be scheduled in the same order as SJF would choose them, the response times will be identical.
Additionally, if all jobs have the same length and the quantum equals the job length, then both SJF and RR will give the same response times since each job completes in exactly one quantum.
